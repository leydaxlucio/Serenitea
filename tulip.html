Mini Fruit Tarts

sweets

The shortbread shells are mildly sweet, which pair well with the rich vanilla custard and fresh tart berries. The mini fruit tarts have a buttery and crumbly crust that practically melts in your mount. The creamy custard has an irresistibly rich texture.

Prep Time

1 hr 30 min

Cook Time

15 min

Total Time

1 hr 45 min

Quantity

14 fruit tarts

Ingredients

Shortbread Crust

1 egg

½ cup sugar

3 tbsp mayonnaise

½ cup butter room temperature

3 tbsp cornstarch

¼ tsp lemon juice

2 cups flour

Vanilla Custard

2 cup milk whole or 2%

6 egg yolks

½ cup sugar

4 tbsp flour

2 tsp vanilla extract

Berries and Fruit Glaze

½ cup apricot preserves jam works too

2 tbsp water

1 cup sliced strawberries

1 cup blueberries

½ cup raspberries

Directions

Step 1

Using a handheld mixer, beat together the egg, sugar, and mayonnaise in a large bowl.

Step 2

Add the flour to the egg and butter mixture and blend on low speed. At this point the dough will become more dense, so it might be easier to just blend it together with your hands.

Step 3

Once the dough is well combined, form it into a ball and cover the bowl with plastic wrap. Then refrigerate for 1 hour.

Step 4

Meanwhile, beat the egg yolks and sugar with a handheld mixer just until combined. Then mix in the flour.

Step 5

Pour 2 cups of milk into a saucepan and bring it to a slight boil (just until bubbles begin to form)

Step 6

Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg yolk mixture while stirring constantly. Make sure you pour very slowly so that the hot milk doesn't cook the eggs.

Step 7

Use a fine mesh sieve to strain the milk and egg mixture back into the saucepan to get rid of any clumps.

Step 8

Place the saucepan back onto the stove and heat it over low heat while stirring constantly. Once it begins to thicken, pour the vanilla in while stirring. You literally don't want to stop stirring or else the custard will become clumpy. Remove the custard from the heat once it's reached a thick and creamy custard consistency.

Step 9

To prevent the custard from forming a skin, place plastic wrap directly on top of the custard, not just on-top of the bowl. Set it aside and let it cool down to room temperature.

Step 10

Scoop out 2 tbsp of the chilled dough into each tartlet tin and press the dough with your fingers so that it reaches all the way up the sides of the tin.

Step 11

Place the tartlets onto a baking sheet and bake for 13 minutes at 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Baking time may vary depending on the oven.

Step 12

Using a cookie scoop, place a dollop of the custard into each tartlets shell.

Step 13

Combine the apricot preserves and water in a small dish and microwave for about 20 seconds then press it through a mini mesh strainer to get rid of any chunks.

Step 14

Arrange the berries onto the custard filled tartlets however you desire and brush with the glaze using a pastry brush.

Glazed Lemon Drop Cookies

sweets

Bite-size Glazed Lemon Drop Cookies are soft not-too-sweet cookies dipped into a sweetly tart glaze.

Prep Time

20 min

Cook Time

10 min

Total Time

30 min

Quantity

36 cookies

Ingredients

Cookie Dough

2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour

2 teaspoons baking powder

¼ teaspoon kosher salt

½ cup butter

1 cup sugar

1 egg

1 teaspoon vanilla

2 tablespoons lemon zest finely minced, about 1 large lemon’s worth

1 tablespoon lemon juice

Glaze

2 cups powdered sugar

2 tablespoons lemon juice

Directions

Step 1

Preheat the oven to 350°F. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt and set aside. Combine the butter and sugar in a mixing bowl and beat until smooth. Add the egg, vanilla, lemon juice and lemon zest. Stir to combine. Add the dry ingredients and mix until combined.

Step 2

The dough will be somewhat dry and crumbly, but can easily be pressed together. Scoop into 1" balls and gently roll them smooth between your hands. Place on a parchment paper-lined baking sheet.

Step 3

Bake for 10 minutes, until the cookies are set, but not browned. These will be small, round cookies and shouldn't flatten or spread much at all. Remove from the oven and transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 4

Slowly add the lemon juice into the powdered sugar, stirring until the glaze can be easily stirred and poured. Drizzle the glaze over the top of each cooled cookie.

Step 5

Allow the glaze to set completely and then transfer the cookies to an airtight container. They should keep well at room temperature for a few days, or in the freezer for up to two months.
