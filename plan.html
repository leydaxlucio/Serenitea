Plan Your Own Tea Party

Tea is meant to be enjoyed with those your like spending time with or even yourself. This is a guide through tea oarty planning and how to enjoy the event.

The Tea Stand

Course 1: Savories and Sandwiches

Course 1

Tea sandwiches and savories are eaten first. These are small bites that can be eaten in 2-3 bites. Eye the sandwiches and savories first before touching one to bring to your plate. You definitely don’t want to be touching anything on the tray that you’re not going to be eating. Eat off your plate. Finish all the tea sandwiches and savories before moving onto the second course.

Course 2

Next course is scones, just touch the scone that you’ll be eating from your plate. Scoop a little of the jam and clotted cream using the spoons that are provided with the condiments and put them on your plate. Knives are used to slather on the jam and cream onto your scones but the scones are eaten with your fingers. Don’t cross-contaminate the jam and cream and the serving spoons. You don’t want to use the cream spoon to scoop the jam. Most importantly, don’t use the serving spoons to put the jam or cream directly on your scone.

Course 3

The sweets are the third and final course. This course is also eaten with your fingers. All pastries and sweets should be small, each one gone in 2-3 bites.
